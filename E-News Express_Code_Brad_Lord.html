E-News Express¶

E-News Express, a news website, wants to expand acquiring new subscribers. Each visitor to the website takes actions based upon their interest. The company wants analyze the interests of potential subscribers to determine whether or not a new feature will be effective. Companies sometimes analyze user responses to two variations of a product to decide which variation is more effective. This technique, known as a/b testing, is used to decide if a new feature is attractive to users based on a chosen metric.¶

The design team of the company has created a new landing page, and they want to know if it is effective to obtain new subscribers. 100 users were randomly selected and divided into two groups - the "control" group was shown the old landing page, while the "treatment" group was shown the new landing page. Various data was collected in order to perform statistical analysis and answer the following questions:¶

  • Do users spend more time on the new landing page?

  • Is the conversion rate (the proportion of users who visit the landing page and subscribe) greater for the new landing page or the old landing page?

  • Does converted status depend on preferred language?

  • Is the mean time spent on the new page the same for different language users?

def bar_perc(plot, feature):
    total = len(feature) # length of the column
    for p in ax.patches:
        percentage = '{:.1f}%'.format(100 * p.get_height()/total) # percentage of each class of the category
        x = p.get_x() + p.get_width() / 2 - 0.05 # width of the plot
        y = p.get_y() + p.get_height()           # height of the plot
        ax.annotate(percentage, (x, y), size = 12) # annotate the percentage

$_a$: 1 < 2

Are T-test assumptions satisfied?¶

  • Continuous data - Yes, the time spent on page is measured on a continuous scale.
  • Normally distributed populations - Yes, populations are assumed to be normal.
  • Independent populations - Random samples are from two different groups, so the samples are from two independent populations.
  • Unequal population standard deviations - The sample standard deviations are different (see groupby above), so the population standard deviations may be assumed to be different.
  • Random sampling from the population - Yes, the collected sample is random.
  • I will use a significance level of 0.05
In [19]:
#import the required functions
from scipy.stats import ttest_ind

#create a new dataset with just the times from the old page, and another one with just the times from the new page
old_time = abtest[abtest['landing_page']=='old']['time_spent_on_the_page']
new_time = abtest[abtest['landing_page']=='new']['time_spent_on_the_page']

# find the p-value
test_stat, p_value = ttest_ind(old_time, new_time, equal_var = False)
print('The p-value is ', p_value)

The p-value is  0.0002784762450333098

Conclusion: since the p-value is significantly less than 0.05, I can reject my null hypothesis. The users spend less time on the old landing page than on the new landing page at a significance level of 0.05¶

I think we should use the two-proportion z-test. Let's see whether the Z-test assumptions are satisfied or not¶

  • Binomally distributed population - Yes, users either converted, or did not convert
  • Random sampling from the population - Yes
  • Can the binomial distribution approximated to normal distribution - Yes. Are np and n(1-p) are greater than or equal to 10?

    $np_1 = 50\cdot \frac{21}{50} =21 \geq 10\\ n(1-p_1) = 50 \cdot \frac{50-21}{50} =29 \geq 10 \\ np_2 = 50 \cdot \frac{33}{50} =33 \geq 10\\ n(1-p_2) = 50 \cdot \frac{50-33}{50} =17 \geq 10 $

$H_0:p_1 =p_2$

$H_a:p_1 < p_2$

Significance level will be 0.05.

Conclusion: since p-value is significantly less than 0.05, I will reject my null hypothesis. The conversion rate for the new landing page is higher than the conversion rate for the old landing page at a significance level of 0.05.¶

Does the converted status depend on the preferred language?¶

I think we should use the Chi-Square test for independence. Let's see whether the assumptions are satisfied or not¶

  • Categorical variables - Yes
  • Value of the number of sample observations in each level of the variable is at least 5 - Yes
  • Random sampling from the population - Yes

$H_0:$ Converted status is independent of preferred language

$H_a:$ Converted status depends on preferred language

Significance level will be 0.05.

Is the mean time spent on the new page same for the different language users?¶

Let 1 = mean amount of time spent on the page for English language users; 2 = the same for French, and 3 = the same for Spanish.

$_0: _1=_2 \text{ and } _1=_3 \text{ and } _2=_3$

$_a: _1\neq_2 \text{ or } _1\neq_3 \text{ or } _2\neq_3$

Significance level is 0.05
